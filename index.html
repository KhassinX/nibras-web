نِبْراس

Nibras

A guiding light for the Qur'an.
Nibras (نِبْراس) means a lamp, a bright light. A reverent reader of the Qur'an — the Arabic always present, a translation of the meaning beside it, and an on-device concordance that surfaces connections in the text. It never interprets and never invents.

Coming to the App Store · Free · No ads · No tracking · Universal Apple

Nibras is built by KhassinX, an independent studio for privacy-first Apple platform apps.

The Qur'an, with reverence

The Arabic, always present

The Qur'an is the Arabic. In Nibras the original Arabic text is always present — never replaced, never paraphrased. The public-domain Arabic text is presented faithfully, verbatim, exactly as transmitted.

A translation of the meaning

Alongside the Arabic, Nibras shows a translation of the meaning — clearly labelled as such. A translation is an interpretation of meaning; it is never called "the Qur'an." The Arabic remains the reference at all times.

A concordance that connects

Ask in plain language and Nibras surfaces related āyāt across the text. The on-device concordance finds connections that are already there — it ranks relevance, it does not author, interpret, or generate a single word.

Reverent by design

Geometry and light only — the aniconic tradition is honoured throughout, with no figurative imagery. Calm, precise, unhurried. Built for reading, study, and reflection, never for spectacle.

It never interprets

No generated text

Nibras uses no generative AI on the sacred text. There is no chatbot speaking for the Qur'an. The concordance surfaces connections within the text; the meaning is left to you and to the scholars.

On your device

Reading and search happen entirely on your device. Your questions, searches, reading position, bookmarks, and notes never leave it — not to us, not to any cloud AI. We have no servers.

No audio, no simulation

Nibras does not synthesize recitation. Tajwīd is a discipline transmitted by qualified reciters; we will not imitate it with a machine. The app presents the written text with reverence.

Transparent sources

Every text is attributed. The Arabic text is provided by Tanzil.net under CC BY 3.0; the Spanish translation of the meaning is by Sheikh Muhammad Isa García, provided via QuranEnc.com / IslamHouse and used under their published terms (he renders the divine name as “Dios”). Provenance is always visible.

Private by architecture

No servers

Nibras has no backend. There is no server to store, see, or breach what you read. Privacy here is not a setting — it is the architecture.

Data Not Collected

Zero analytics. Zero advertising. Zero tracking. The App Privacy Label reads "Data Not Collected," because nothing is collected.

Free, pro bono

Nibras is free, with no ads and no subscription. A small optional tip jar exists for those who wish to support the work — entirely optional, never required.

Universal Apple

Nibras is built for iPhone, iPad, Mac, and Apple Watch, on a foundation of iOS 26 Liquid Glass. Available in English and Spanish at launch, with the Arabic text always present.
